Transportation

Public

Public transportation is available through the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA). They provide the Metrobus and Metro Rail which runs from 5 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. Monday - Thursday, 5 a.m. to 1 a.m. Friday, 7 a.m. to 1 a.m. on Saturday and 8a.m. to 11 p.m. on Sundays. They include stops at many popular locations inculding the Smithsonian, Woodley Park-Zoo and Chinatown. Payments must be made with a rechargable SmarTrip card.

The DC Circulator is another option for those looking to use the bus. They have six specific routes that go through the National Mall and neighborhoods including Adams Morgan, Georgetown, and Capitol Hill. Each ride is only $1, and the buses run every 10 minutes.

Ride-sharing
Ride sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft , are avialable as well. Via is a carpooling service available in the D.C. area that operates 24/7 and rides start at $3.65

Taxis & Rental Cars

Finding a taxi in D.C. is easy, especially since there are over 6,000 taxis operating in the city. Information on taxi fares can be found here. If you prefer to rent your own car, there are apps like ParkWhiz and Spot Hero that help you find and reserve parking spot for wherever you need to go.

Bikes, Walking & Segways

Washington DC has wide sidewalks allowing you to view the city on foot or bike. Bike and Roll and Capital Bikeshare offer bike rentals to get around the city at different rates.
